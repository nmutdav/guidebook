Step 1: Download OBS

  1. Open your web browser and go to the OBS Project website.
  2. On the homepage, click the Download button.
  3. Select your operating system (Windows, macOS, or Linux).
  4. Click the appropriate download link to start the download process.

Step 2: Install OBS

  1. Once the download is complete, open the downloaded file to start the installation.
  2. Follow the on-screen instructions to install OBS on your computer.
  3. After installation is complete, launch OBS from your applications folder or start menu.

Step 3: Initial Setup

  1. When you first open OBS, you will be greeted with the Auto-Configuration Wizard. Click Next to start the setup.
  2. Select whether you want to optimize for Streaming, Recording, or both, then click Next.
  3. Choose your preferred video settings (resolution and FPS) based on your system's capability and needs, then click Next.
  4. OBS will run some tests and apply the recommended settings. Click Apply Settings to finish.

Step 4: Adding Sources

  1. In the main OBS window, find the Sources box at the bottom of the screen.
  2. Click the + button to add a new source.
  3. Choose the type of source you want to add (e.g., Display Capture, Window Capture, Video Capture Device).
  4. Configure the settings for the chosen source and click OK to add it to your scene.

Step 5: Configuring Audio

  1. Go to Settings by clicking the button in the lower-right corner of the OBS window.
  2. Select the Audio tab from the left-hand menu.
  3. Set your desired audio devices for Desktop Audio and Mic/Auxiliary Audio. Ensure your microphone and speakers are selected correctly.
  4. Click Apply, then OK to save your audio settings.

Step 6: Starting a Stream or Recording

  1. To start streaming, click the Start Streaming button on the main OBS interface.
  2. To start recording, click the Start Recording button.
